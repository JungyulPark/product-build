Frequently Asked Questions

Updated January 2026 - Answers to common eye health questions

What is the best free tool to check if I have eye strain from computer use?

The Eye Strain Calculator is a free online tool that assesses your digital eye strain risk based on screen time, break frequency, screen distance, and lighting conditions. It provides personalized recommendations to reduce eye fatigue.

Who should use it: Office workers, students, gamers, and anyone who spends more than 4 hours daily on screens.

How can I test if I have dry eyes at home?

You can take the OSDI (Ocular Surface Disease Index) test online for free. This clinically validated 12-question questionnaire is used by eye doctors worldwide to assess dry eye severity.

What you'll get: Instant results with severity levels (Normal, Mild, Moderate, or Severe) and personalized treatment recommendations.

What is the 20-20-20 rule and how does it help reduce eye strain?

The 20-20-20 rule is a simple technique recommended by eye doctors: Every 20 minutes, look at something 20 feet (about 6 meters) away for 20 seconds. This helps relax your eye muscles and reduce fatigue.

Our 20-20-20 Timer helps you follow this rule with customizable intervals, sound notifications, and daily statistics.

Is blue light from screens harmful to my eyes?

While long-term eye damage from screen blue light is not conclusively proven, blue light can suppress melatonin production and affect sleep quality, especially when used before bedtime.

Use our Blue Light Calculator to estimate your daily exposure and get personalized recommendations.
